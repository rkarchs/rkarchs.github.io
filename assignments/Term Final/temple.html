Temple Background

The Washington D.C. Temple was the first temple built in the eastern United States and was the 16th of the Church when President Spencer W. Kimball dedicated it on November 19, 1974.

At 288 feet, it is the tallest temple, and it is the only temple outside of Utah to have six ordinance rooms.

The temple serves Latter-day Saints in Washington, D.C., Pennsylvania, Virginia, West Virginia, Maryland, Delaware, and New Jersey.

Temple Background

The San Diego California Temple was the 45th temple of the Church when President Gordon B. Hinckley, then first counselor in the First Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, dedicated it on April 25, 1993.

It was the third temple built in California, following the Los Angeles California Temple and the Oakland California Temple.

The temple serves Latter-day Saints in the San Diego area and northwestern Mexico.

Temple Background

The Manhattan New York Temple was the 119th temple of the Church when President Gordon B. Hinckley dedicated it on June 13, 2004.

The Manhattan New York Temple was the first ever in the New York metro region and serves more than 42,000 church members in New York, New Jersey and Connecticut.
